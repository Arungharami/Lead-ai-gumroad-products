# 04 — Lead Research Workflow

You do not need expensive prospecting software or email search databases (like Apollo, ZoomInfo, or Lusha) to find local business leads. This step-by-step workflow teaches you how to identify high-converting prospects and find decision-maker emails using free, public online directories.

---

## The 4-Step Prospecting Workflow

### Step 1: Search Local Niches on Google Maps
1. Open Google Maps.
2. Search for your target niche within a specific city (e.g. "Residential HVAC in Portland" or "Family Law firms in Sacramento").
3. Scroll past the sponsored ads to check the organic business listings.
4. Open the first 20 listings and inspect their website links.

### Step 2: Spot the Digital Gaps (The Website Audit)
Open each website and check for these three specific conversion rate leaks:
- **No Chat Option**: Is there any interactive chat widget (live chat or chatbot) present on the landing page?
- **Static Forms**: Does their "Contact Us" or "Schedule" link point to a long static form (containing 5+ required inputs) that discourages mobile users?
- **Contact Friction**: Is their phone number or booking availability limited to business hours (e.g. 8 AM - 5 PM)? If a homeowner has a plumbing leak at 9 PM, they cannot book an appointment instantly.

*Highlight any business that has high Google Reviews (30+) but has a sluggish, outdated website with none of these interactive elements. They have high search intent but are dropping leads.*

### Step 3: Locate the Decision Maker & Contact Info
Before sending any email, you must find the decision-maker's name and direct contact details.

1. **Find the Name**: Open their website's "About Us", "Our Team", or "Meet the Staff" pages. Identify the owner, founder, partner, or general manager.
2. **Locate the Email**: 
   - Check their website footer or contact pages for direct emails (e.g. `owner@company.com` or `info@company.com`).
   - If they only list a phone number, search their official business page on LinkedIn to identify the owner's profile.
3. **Verify the Email**: Use a free email validation tool (such as Hunter.io or NeverBounce's free checker) to confirm the address is active and correct.

### Step 4: Populate Your CRM
Open `09-simple-crm-tracker.csv` in Excel or your markdown editor. For each qualified prospect, fill in:
- Business Name
- Website URL
- Decision Maker's Name
- Email Address
- Specific Lead Leak Found (e.g. "Long 8-field static booking form, no off-hour scheduling option")

---

## Action Goal: The 10-Prospect List

Before sending your outreach, build a list of **10 highly qualified, pre-audited prospects** using this exact workflow. 

By taking the time to research specific leaks beforehand, your outreach messages will stand out from 99% of generic spam emails.
