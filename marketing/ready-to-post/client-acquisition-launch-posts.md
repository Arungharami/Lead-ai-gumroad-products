## LinkedIn Post 2 — Value Post

If you want to sell AI automation to local businesses, do not start with “I can build you an AI chatbot.”

Start with a business problem.

Examples:

- Restaurant: missing catering inquiries
- Salon: no online booking flow
- Clinic: repeated FAQ calls
- Barbershop: Instagram DMs not answered fast enough
- Retail store: no lead capture on website
- Local service: quote requests are not organized

Then offer a small automation:

- FAQ chatbot
- Booking assistant
- Lead capture form
- Follow-up message flow
- Review request automation

AI services become easier to sell when the message is simple:

“I noticed this problem. I can help you fix this part of your customer flow.”

## Reddit-Style Value Post

Title: I made a simple workflow for finding small businesses that may need AI automation

Post:

I noticed a common problem among AI beginners and freelancers: they learn tools, prompts, and chatbot builders, but they do not know how to find actual business problems.

The workflow I use is simple:

1. Pick one local niche, such as salons, restaurants, clinics, barbershops, or local service businesses.
2. Review the business website and public profiles.
3. Look for simple issues: no booking flow, weak FAQ, no lead capture, slow response channels, no follow-up.
4. Write a short personalized message about that specific issue.
5. Offer one small automation, not a huge AI transformation.

Example:

“I noticed your website has service information, but I did not see a simple booking or question form. I help local businesses add simple AI-assisted lead capture and FAQ flows so customers can ask questions and request appointments more easily.”

I turned this into a Gumroad product with worksheets, audit prompts, outreach templates, follow-ups, discovery script, proposal template, and CRM tracker.

Not a guaranteed income product. Just a practical workflow for people trying to take action.
