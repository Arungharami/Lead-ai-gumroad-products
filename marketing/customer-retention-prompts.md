# Customer Retention Prompts — Lead.AI

**Goal:** Keep existing buyers engaged, informed, and moving through the product ladder.

---

## Prompt 1: Product update announcement

```
Write a short email announcing an update to [PRODUCT NAME].

What is new in this version: [LIST OF CHANGES]
Version number: [VERSION NUMBER]
Who receives this: existing buyers of [PRODUCT NAME]

Requirements:
- Subject: specific about what improved (not just "New update!")
- Under 150 words
- Explain what changed and the benefit to the buyer
- Re-download instructions: "Visit your Gumroad library to get the update"
- Personal, warm tone — not automated marketing copy
```

---

## Prompt 2: Check-in email (30 days after purchase)

```
Write a 30-day check-in email for buyers of [PRODUCT NAME].

Goal: Show the buyer you care about their progress, offer help, and create 
awareness of the next product.

Requirements:
- Subject: asks about their specific goal (related to the product they bought)
- Under 150 words
- Ask one specific question about their progress
- Offer one specific piece of help (not just "let me know if you need anything")
- One sentence about the natural next product: "[Product] would be a good next step if you want to [result]"
- Personal sign-off from Arun
```

---

## Prompt 3: "What I am building next" transparency email

```
Write an email sharing what is being built next for Lead.AI.

What is actually in progress: [DESCRIBE HONESTLY what the next product or update is]

Requirements:
- Subject: honest preview of what is coming (no fake hype)
- Under 200 words
- Share what is being built and why
- Invite buyers to tell you what they want to see (genuine request)
- This builds trust — buyers who feel included in the journey are more likely to buy again
- No promises you cannot keep
```

---

## Prompt 4: Re-engagement email for inactive buyers

```
Write a re-engagement email for a buyer who purchased [PRODUCT NAME] 60+ days ago 
but has not returned.

Requirements:
- Subject: asks a direct question about whether they used it
- Under 150 words
- No guilt-tripping — just genuine curiosity about whether it was useful
- Offer to help with whatever stopped them
- If they did not use it at all, offer an alternative: "If [this product] was not 
  the right fit for where you are right now, [other product] might be closer to your 
  current need"
- Personal, honest, no pressure
```

---

## Prompt 5: Bundle upsell email for single-product buyers

```
Write an email to a buyer of [INDIVIDUAL PRODUCT] introducing the Lead.AI bundle.

Context:
- They bought: [PRODUCT NAME] at [PRICE]
- Bundle includes their product + 3 more + 3 bonuses
- Bundle price: $79
- What they save by upgrading: [$79 minus what they paid, minus the price of items they do not have]

Requirements:
- Subject: honest about the bundle (not "YOU ARE MISSING OUT")
- Acknowledge their existing purchase: "Since you already have [product], 
  upgrading to the bundle costs less for you"
- Under 200 words
- Specific about what they get that they do not already have
- No fake urgency
- Email me at a.gharami.325@westcliff.edu to arrange a discount for existing buyers
```
