# LinkedIn Post Prompts — Lead.AI

**Instructions:** Use these prompts in ChatGPT or Claude to generate LinkedIn posts. Replace [BRACKETED] fields with your specifics before running the prompt. Review and edit the output — it should sound like you, not like an AI.

---

## Prompt 1: Educational post — explain one AI concept

```
Write a LinkedIn post explaining [AI CONCEPT, e.g., "what SHAP values are"] 
for an audience of AI students, beginner developers, and startup founders.

Requirements:
- Opening line must not start with "I" and must make a specific claim or ask a question
- Explain the concept simply, using a real-world analogy
- Include one practical example a reader could apply today
- 200–300 words
- End with one question to prompt comments
- Tone: knowledgeable but not academic. Builder talking to builders.
- No hashtag walls — maximum 3 hashtags at the end
- Do not mention my product in the post itself
```

---

## Prompt 2: Story post — something you learned building this

```
Write a LinkedIn post about [A SPECIFIC LESSON I LEARNED while building my AI products].

Context: I am an AI Engineer and Applied Researcher who built [SPECIFIC PRODUCT, e.g., 
"a fraud detection system with SHAP explainability"]. The lesson I want to share is:
[SPECIFIC LESSON, e.g., "that class imbalance handling matters more than model choice 
for fraud detection"].

Requirements:
- Open with the outcome or surprise, not the backstory
- Be specific — numbers, tools, real results (only real ones)
- 200–250 words
- End with one actionable takeaway for the reader
- Natural, personal voice — not polished corporate speak
- 2–3 hashtags maximum
```

---

## Prompt 3: Product launch post

```
Write a LinkedIn post announcing [PRODUCT NAME] — an honest, helpful announcement 
that does not sound like an ad.

Product: [PRODUCT NAME]
What it is: [ONE SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Who it is for: [SPECIFIC AUDIENCE]
Price: [PRICE]
Link: [GUMROAD LINK]

Requirements:
- Lead with the problem it solves, not the product
- Be specific about what the buyer gets
- Do NOT use phrases like: "I am excited to share", "game-changer", "proud to announce"
- 200 words maximum
- One clear CTA at the end with the link
- 2–3 hashtags maximum
- Tone: honest, direct, builder talking to fellow builders
```

---

## Prompt 4: Value post — share a useful framework

```
Write a LinkedIn post sharing [A FRAMEWORK OR CHECKLIST related to AI product building].

The framework I want to share: [DESCRIBE THE FRAMEWORK — e.g., "how to evaluate 
whether a business problem is worth solving with AI vs. without AI"]

Requirements:
- Format as a numbered list (3–5 items)
- Each item is specific and immediately useful
- Brief intro sentence (1–2 sentences)
- Brief close that ties it together (1 sentence)
- 200–300 words total
- No product pitch in the post itself
- 2–3 hashtags at the end
```

---

## Prompt 5: Credibility post — honest origin story

```
Write a short LinkedIn post explaining why I built [PRODUCT NAME].

Context: 
- My background: [BRIEF BACKGROUND]
- The real problem I was trying to solve: [PROBLEM]
- What the product does: [WHAT IT DOES IN ONE SENTENCE]
- What I want readers to take away: [WHAT I HOPE THIS POST CONVEYS]

Requirements:
- Honest — no hype, no exaggerated claims
- Personal — why I specifically built this, not just "there was a market need"
- 150–200 words
- No hard sell. The product link is in the comments, not the post.
- End with an invitation for people to share their own related experience
```

---

## Prompt 6: Engagement post — ask a question

```
Write a LinkedIn question post to start a conversation with [SPECIFIC AUDIENCE, 
e.g., "freelance data scientists" or "startup founders evaluating AI tools"].

Topic: [TOPIC, e.g., "what is the hardest part of explaining your ML model to 
a non-technical client?"]

Requirements:
- Open with the question or a one-sentence setup + the question
- Add 2–3 sentences of your own perspective on the topic (honest, specific)
- End by asking the audience to share their experience
- 100–150 words maximum
- Not a product pitch — purely community engagement
```

---

## Post frequency guide

- 3–5 posts per week is optimal for LinkedIn algorithm
- Mix: 2 educational posts, 1 story/credibility post, 1 engagement question post, 1 product-related post
- Never post more than 1 product announcement per week
- Reply to every comment within 24 hours
