### Email 2 — Day 1

```
Write a follow-up email to someone who downloaded a free AI product launch 
checklist 24 hours ago.

Context:
- The free resource they downloaded: a checklist for launching an AI digital product
- My name: Arun Kumar Gharami
- My brand: Lead.AI
- My email: a.gharami.325@westcliff.edu

Email requirements:
- Subject line: asks a specific question about the checklist (not "Did you open it?")
- Under 150 words
- Personal tone — like a founder following up, not a marketing automation
- One specific, useful tip related to Phase 1 of the checklist
- No product pitch in this email
- CTA: reply with a question if they have one
```

---

### Email 3 — Day 3

```
Write an email to a free checklist downloader on Day 3 of the follow-up sequence.

Goal: Deliver standalone value and create awareness of the paid $10 playbook.

Email requirements:
- Subject line: a specific insight or tip (not "Check this out")
- Content: one specific, useful insight about launching AI products — something 
  that would be useful even to someone who never buys anything
- 150–200 words
- Natural mention of the $10 playbook: honest, not pushy. 
  Example: "If you want the full system behind this, the $10 AI Business Playbook 
  covers it in depth: [link]"
- No urgency language, no scarcity
```

---

### Email 4 — Day 5

```
Write an email to a free checklist downloader on Day 5.

Goal: Share a use case that feels relevant to this audience (AI students, freelancers, 
founders) — not a testimonial, just a honest description of how someone in that 
situation would use the product.

Audience: AI students, beginner developers, freelancers who want to offer AI services.

Email requirements:
- Subject line: the use case in one line (e.g., "How a freelancer uses this to pitch clients")
- 150–200 words
- Honest, specific, no fake social proof
- Links to the $10 playbook and the $19 prompt vault as relevant options
- Personal sign-off
```

---

### Email 5 — Day 7

```
Write the final email in a 7-day welcome sequence for a free checklist downloader.

Goal: Introduce the full Lead.AI bundle as the natural next step for serious buyers.

Email requirements:
- Subject line: honest description of the bundle (not "LAST CHANCE" or fake urgency)
- 200 words maximum
- Clear list of what is in the bundle (4 products + 3 bonuses)
- Honest price: $79 launch price
- No: fake deadline, fake scarcity, fake testimonials
- Personal tone — founder talking to a real person
- CTA: one clear link to the bundle
- Sign-off: mention ongoing email value — this is not the last email
```

### Post-purchase email — Day 3

```
Write a follow-up email to a buyer 3 days after purchasing [PRODUCT NAME].

Goal: Check in, offer help, and plant the seed for the next product.

Context:
- Product purchased: [PRODUCT NAME]
- What the buyer should have accomplished by Day 3: [BUYER'S DAY 3 GOAL from onboarding guide]
- Natural next product in the upsell path: [NEXT PRODUCT NAME AND LINK]

Email requirements:
- Subject line: specific to their product and what they should be doing
- Under 150 words
- Ask how it is going — genuine interest
- Offer to help with the most common Day 3 question
- One natural mention of the next product: "When you are ready for [next step], [product] is the tool"
- No pressure
```

---

### Product update email (when a new version is released)

```
Write a product update email announcing [PRODUCT NAME] version [VERSION NUMBER].

What changed:
- [LIST OF CHANGES]

Email requirements:
- Subject line: what improved (not just "update available")
- Under 200 words
- Explain what changed and why it matters to the buyer
- Clear re-download instructions: "Go to your Gumroad library to download the update"
- Thank existing buyers for their support
- If relevant, mention an upsell briefly (not as the main point)
```

- Send from a.gharami.325@westcliff.edu or via Gumroad's email tool
- Maximum 2 emails per week to any subscriber
- Plain text emails often outperform HTML emails for personal brands
- Always include an unsubscribe note (Gumroad handles this automatically)
- Reply to every reply personally — this is still a small enough business for that
